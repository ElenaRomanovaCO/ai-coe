# NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

A voluntary framework for managing AI risks across the lifecycle. Not law, but widely
referenced as a baseline for trustworthy-AI practices.

## Core functions

- **Govern** — Cultivate a risk-aware culture; assign accountability.
- **Map** — Establish context and identify risks for the specific use case.
- **Measure** — Analyze and track risks with appropriate metrics.
- **Manage** — Prioritize and act on risks; monitor over time.

## Trustworthiness characteristics

Valid and reliable, safe, secure and resilient, accountable and transparent,
explainable and interpretable, privacy-enhanced, and fair with harmful bias managed.

## What this means for engagements

Use the four functions as the backbone of an AI governance operating model. Map each
use case's risks before build, define measurement up front, and assign clear
ownership. Pairs well with the Governance & Risk Checker and Ethics modules.
