# California SB-1001 — Bot Disclosure

California law making it unlawful to use a bot to communicate with a person in the
state to incentivize a sale or influence a vote without disclosing that it is a bot,
in certain contexts.

## Key points

- Applies to bots used to knowingly deceive about artificial identity for commercial
  transactions or electoral influence.
- Disclosure must be clear and conspicuous.
- Aimed at public-facing platforms of meaningful scale.

## What this means for engagements

Any customer-facing conversational AI that nudges toward a purchase should clearly
disclose that the user is interacting with an automated system. Build the disclosure
into the assistant's first message rather than burying it. This complements the EU AI
Act's limited-risk transparency duty — a single clear "you're chatting with an AI"
pattern satisfies both.
