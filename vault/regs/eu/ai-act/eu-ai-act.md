# EU Artificial Intelligence Act

The EU's horizontal AI regulation, structured around risk tiers. Obligations scale
with the risk a system poses.

## Risk tiers

- **Unacceptable** — Banned practices (e.g. social scoring, certain biometric uses).
- **High-risk** — Systems in sensitive domains (medical devices, credit, employment,
  critical infrastructure). Subject to the heaviest obligations.
- **Limited** — Transparency duties (e.g. disclosing that a user is interacting with
  AI, labeling synthetic content).
- **Minimal** — Most systems; largely unregulated.

## High-risk obligations (summary)

Risk management, data governance, technical documentation, logging, human oversight,
accuracy/robustness, and conformity assessment before market placement.

## What this means for engagements

Classify each use case by tier early. For high-risk systems, plan documentation,
human oversight, and conformity steps into the delivery timeline from the start.
Limited-risk systems still need clear AI-interaction disclosure. Confirm current
applicability dates, which phase in over time.
